# VRE Construction — Cold Call Script

**Audience:** Cincinnati real-estate investors, flippers, landlords, GCs,
property managers, and homeowners doing rehabs.
**Goal of the call:** book a free 15-minute walkthrough OR get permission
to text/email the instant-quote tool link. Not selling on the phone.
**Time budget:** 90 seconds max before they say yes or no.

---

## 0. Before you dial

Have these open:
- Lead list (name, role, phone, source)
- Notebook / CRM tab
- This script
- A working phone number you'd answer back from

**Mindset:** You're not begging for work. You're calling because you do
something the average sparky around here doesn't — pull permits, carry
real GL, hit a deadline, give a written line-itemized estimate. They will
either need that or they won't. You're qualifying *them*.

---

## 1. Opening (10 seconds)

> "Hey, is this {first name}? — This is {your name} with VRE
> Construction here in Cincinnati. Quick reason for the call, then I'll
> get out of your hair: do you work with a permitted electrician right
> now for your {flips / rentals / rehabs}?"

**Why this works:** introduces who, where, why in one breath. Ends on
a closed question that gets a real answer.

**If they say YES → go to §2A.**
**If they say NO → go to §2B.**
**If they say "what's this about?" → §3.**

---

## 2A. They have one already

> "Cool — last thing, then I'm done. If yours ever gets booked out two
> weeks during a draw window, can I text you a 60-second instant-quote
> tool we built? You punch in panel/EV/wiring, it spits back a range and
> a PDF. Costs you nothing to have in your back pocket."

**If yes:** "What's the best cell?" → text them
`https://vre-construction.com/quote` within 5 min.

**If no:** "Totally fair. Good luck on the project — bye."

---

## 2B. They don't have one / they use whoever's cheap

> "Got it. Two reasons we might be worth a 15-minute call: we pull
> permits in our name and we line-item the estimate so it drops straight
> into a draw schedule or a lender package. After three jobs, we do a
> quiet rebate on labor. I won't get into that on the phone — but if any
> of that sounds useful, can I send you a quote tool you can play with
> tonight?"

**Two paths:**
- **Wants the tool now:** "What's the best cell? You'll get a text from
  513-555-0118 in five minutes."
- **Wants to talk first:** "Got time tomorrow morning or Thursday for a
  15-minute walkthrough at the property? No charge, no commitment."

---

## 3. "What's this about?" (skeptical opener)

> "Fair. Short version: I'm with VRE Construction, we're a family-owned
> electrical shop in Cincinnati. We focus on flip and rental work
> because the timelines are tighter and the math has to be cleaner. We
> built a free instant-quote tool — you put in the scope, it gives you
> a real range. I'm calling people who do volume to see if it's useful
> in your workflow. Are you working flips right now, or rentals,
> or both?"

Then back to §2A or §2B based on the answer.

---

## 4. Objection handlers

**"I have a guy."**
> "Smart. Mine's a backup play. If yours ever bails, you'll wish you had
> our number. Want me to text it so you have it?"

**"You're too expensive."**
> "You haven't seen a price yet. The tool gives you a range in 60 seconds
> — try it before you decide we're expensive. Text or email?"

**"Send me an email."**
> "Done — what's the address? You'll get one note from
> hello@vre-construction.com today with the tool link, nothing else.
> Promise."
(After call: send via the contact form on vre-construction.com so the
lead lands in the CRM.)

**"Are you licensed?"**
> "Yes — licensed electrical in Hamilton County, $2M GL, we pull permits
> in our name. Want me to send the COI with the quote?"

**"How long have you been doing this?"**
> "Honest answer — VRE the business is new, the trade isn't. {Owner} has
> been wiring in Cincinnati for {N} years. We're a small crew because
> we'd rather do the job right than scale fast. That's why we line-item
> everything and give you a 30-day price hold."

**"Why are you calling me?"**
> "{Lead source — Zillow listing / referral / public flip permit /
> driving for dollars}. You looked like you do volume, so I wanted to
> get on your radar before you needed us."

**"Take me off your list."**
> "Done. Have a good one." → mark `DNC` in CRM, do not call again.

---

## 5. The investor rebate (only after they're warm)

If — and only if — they're clearly interested, they do volume, AND they
ask "what's the catch / what's the deal":

> "After your third booked job with us, we credit 8 to 12 percent of the
> labor on the next bid. We don't publish that anywhere. It rewards
> people who actually move volume with us instead of shopping us every
> time."

**Never lead with this.** It's a closer, not an opener.

---

## 6. Close (10 seconds)

**Always end the call with one of three things:**
1. **A scheduled walkthrough** (best): "{Day} at {time}, the property at
   {address}. I'll text confirmation."
2. **Permission to text the tool** (good): "Text incoming from
   513-555-0118 in 5 minutes."
3. **Permission to email** (acceptable): "One email from
   hello@vre-construction.com today, nothing after that without your
   say-so."

If you don't have one of those three, the call didn't count.

---

## 7. After every call — log in CRM

- Name, phone, role (owner / investor / GC / PM)
- Source (where the lead came from)
- Outcome (scheduled / texted tool / emailed / DNC / no answer)
- Next action + date
- One-line note: their actual words about pain or hesitation

Without this, the script is just talking. With it, you can pattern-match
what's working in a week.

---

## 8. Daily targets (first 30 days)

- **30 dials/day** (real dials, not "tried twice and gave up")
- 5–8 actual conversations
- 1–2 walkthroughs booked OR 3–4 tool-link texts sent
- 1 follow-up call on every "send me info" from last week

If you hit those numbers and nothing closes after 30 days, the script
isn't the problem — the lead list is. Call me.

---

## 9. Voicemail (when no answer)

> "Hey {first name}, this is {your name} with VRE Construction in
> Cincinnati — we do permitted electrical for flips and rentals. Got a
> 60-second quote tool I'd like you to have in your back pocket whether
> you ever use us or not. I'll text you the link from 513-555-0118 — no
> reply needed if it's not useful. Talk soon."

Then text the link.

---

## 10. Lead sources (if you don't have a list yet)

In Hamilton County alone, public/free sources for the first 200 dials:
- **Zillow / Redfin** — "for sale by owner" + "fixer-upper" filters →
  agent contact, sometimes seller direct
- **Hamilton County Auditor public records** — recent sub-$120k cash
  purchases = flippers
- **BiggerPockets local forum** — Cincinnati investor meet-up lists
- **Permit search (City of Cincinnati building permits)** — anyone who
  pulled an electrical or remodel permit in the last 60 days
- **Facebook groups:** "Cincinnati Real Estate Investors,"
  "Cincinnati Flippers"
- **Driving for dollars** — visible rehabs, knock or skip-trace
- **Property managers** — every property management company in town
  needs an electrician; they don't usually have a primary

Start with permit search and the auditor records. Those are people with
money already in motion.
