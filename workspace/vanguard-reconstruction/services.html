What I do, and what I don't.

A clear scope is professional courtesy. It saves your time and mine, and it means the work that lands on my truck is work I'm fast and accurate at.

In scope

  • Panel upgrades and service changes
  • Sub-panels and feeder runs
  • Breaker swaps, AFCI/GFCI updates
  • New circuits and dedicated lines
  • 240V drops (EV chargers, dryers, ranges)
  • Outlets, switches, dimmers
  • Fixture installs and ceiling fans
  • Whole-house surge protection
  • Fault tracing and troubleshooting
  • Knob-and-tube assessments
  • Pre-purchase electrical walkthroughs

Not my scope

  • Drywall, framing, finish carpentry
  • Plumbing rough or repair
  • HVAC install or service
  • Painting, flooring, tile
  • Roofing, siding, gutters
  • Generator install (gas hook-up side)
  • Low-voltage / structured cabling builds
  • Solar & battery systems
  • Anything 5+ units or commercial code
  • New construction GC work
Why the line is drawn here.
I have fifteen years across most of the trades on that "not my scope" list. I can do them. I'm not the fastest or most accurate person at them, and pretending otherwise priced my time wrong for years. Specializing means the panel goes in clean the first time, the inspector signs it off, and the bill is honest.

Service area

Cincinnati and the surrounding residential market. Single-family homes through 4-unit buildings — the segment that runs on residential code, not commercial. If your job is bigger than that, I'll tell you straight and point you to someone licensed for it.

Pricing

Flat-rate quotes for defined work (panels, circuits, fixture installs). Time-and-materials for diagnostics and open-ended troubleshooting, with a cap agreed up front. No recurring fees, no membership upsells, no surprise trip charges.
