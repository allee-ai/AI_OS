# Cold-Call Cheat Sheet — Vanguard Reconstruction
## Target: Cincinnati landlords & investors with 1–3 family residential

Print this. Tape it to the wall next to the phone.

---

## Opener (15 seconds, no longer)

> "Hey [name], this is [you] with Vanguard Reconstruction in Cincinnati — we're residential electricians, single-family through 3-family. Most of our work is panel upgrades, EV drops, and turnover electrical for landlords. Quick question: who do you call when something electrical pops on one of your properties?"

**Why it works:**
- Names the niche in the first sentence (residential electrical, ≤3-family, landlord-focused) → instant qualification
- Lists the three highest-paying, highest-pain jobs (panels, EV, turn electrical) → one will resonate
- Ends with a question they can answer in 2 seconds → keeps them on the line

**Variants if the first one's not landing:**
- *"…we specialize in panel swaps and pre-purchase electrical walkthroughs — does either of those come up much in your portfolio?"*
- *"…we just finished a 200-amp panel swap on a duplex in [neighborhood] for a landlord like you. Wanted to see if you have anything similar coming up."*
- *"…tenants asking about EV chargers yet? We're doing more of those drops every month."*

## Objection → counter

| What they say | What you say |
|---|---|
| "We have an electrician." | "Totally fair — most of our clients call us as their #2 when their #1 is booked or slow on quotes. Send me the next thing they don't pick up on. Mind if I email our packet so you have us in your back pocket?" |
| "We're not looking right now." | "No problem. When a tenant calls about a dead circuit or you need a panel for a closing, response time matters more than price — can I send you a one-pager with our pricing bands so you have it when something pops?" |
| "Send me an email." | "Will do — packet's already drafted. What's the best email? And while I have you: what's your bigger headache — finding someone who picks up, or finding someone who quotes flat-rate?" *(captures intel even on a brush-off)* |
| "How much for a panel?" | "100-amp to 200-amp like-for-like runs $[X]–$[Y] flat-rate, one day. Mast and grounding update adds [$X]. I'll send the full price band sheet — what's the best email?" |
| "Are you licensed and insured?" | "Yes — Ohio EL license, $[N]M GL, workers' comp on the crew. I'll include the certs in the packet. What's the best email?" |
| "I don't have time." | "Hear you. 30 seconds: text 'PACKET' to [YOUR CELL] and I'll send everything. No call needed." *(gives them an out that still captures them)* |
| "We only use big electrical companies." | "Makes sense for commercial. We're built for the 1–3 family side — flat-rate, one point of contact, no service-fee runaround. Mind if I send our packet for the rental-side pile?" |
| "How fast can you start?" | "Live fault with no power: same day. Standard panel or circuit work: usually within the week of a signed quote. Send me an address and I'll come back with a date." |
| "Do you do drywall / plumbing / [non-electrical]?" | "We don't — that's a different trade. We're electrical only and we'll point you to good crews for the rest. Want me to send the panel/circuit/EV pricing in the meantime?" |
| "We have 5+ unit buildings." | "We stop at 3-family — different code, different inspectors, honest about it. If you have any 1–3s in the portfolio, the offer stands. If not, no worries." |
| "What about pre-purchase inspections?" | "Yes — we do pre-purchase electrical walkthroughs with a written report. Use it for your offer or your reno budget. $[X]. When's your next closing?" |

## Information to capture on every live call (5 seconds at the end)

Before you hang up, get these or note "—":

- **Name + role** (owner-operator, PM, investor, agent?)
- **Portfolio size** ("how many properties? how many units total?")
- **Their #1 electrical pain** (panel age? tenant complaints? EV demand? insurance pushback?)
- **Best email**
- **Permission to send packet** (yes/no/maybe)
- **Any property they're closing on soon** (pre-purchase walkthrough is the easy yes)
- **Follow-up timing** ("worth a check-in in 30 days?")

If you got the email, the call paid for itself. Send the packet within 5 minutes — while you're still in their head.

---

## Close patterns ranked by ease

1. **Email the packet** — easiest yes; gets you in their inbox forever
2. **Pre-purchase walkthrough** — if they mention a closing, push for the date; cheap entry, high trust
3. **Same-day quote from photos** — if they mention a real issue, ask for panel photos before you hang up
4. **15-min site visit** — for warm ones with multiple things to look at
5. **Booked panel/circuit job** — only on a true emergency or strong referral

Don't push for #5 on a cold call. Aim for #1 every time. #2–5 happen on follow-up.

## Quick fact ammo (for when they pause)

- Insurance carriers are dropping policies on FPE, Zinsco, and Federal Pioneer panels. Many landlords don't know they have one. Ask: *"Do you know what panel brand is on your properties?"*
- Section 8 inspections fail on missing GFCI in kitchens/baths and on open junctions. Easy win to mention.
- Cincinnati area knob-and-tube is everywhere in pre-1950 stock. *"If any of your properties are pre-war, we should walk them."*
- Tenants asking for EV chargers is a 2025–2026 trend, not a 2030 problem. *"We can quote a 240V drop in 24 hours."*

---

## What kills the call (don't do)

- Talking about yourself for more than 15 seconds before asking a question
- Listing services without tying them to a pain
- Quoting hard numbers on a panel without panel photos
- Promising work outside electrical scope
- "Just checking in" without a reason — always have a hook (panel-brand recall, recent job nearby, EV trend, insurance trend)
