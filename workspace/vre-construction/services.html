In scope · not our scope

Specializing means the panel goes in clean the first time, the work holds up, and the bill is honest. If your job sits in the right column, we'll say so on the first call and refer you to someone good — saves a wasted site visit.

In scope

  • Panel upgrades and service changes (100A → 200A → 400A)
  • Sub-panels and feeder runs
  • Breaker swaps, AFCI/GFCI updates
  • New circuits and dedicated lines (kitchen, bath, laundry, office)
  • 240V drops (EV chargers, dryers, ranges, hot tubs)
  • Outlets, switches, dimmers, smart switches
  • Fixture installs and ceiling fans
  • Whole-house surge protection
  • Fault tracing and troubleshooting
  • Knob-and-tube assessments and remediation
  • Pre-purchase electrical walkthroughs
  • Smoke / CO detector hardwiring & interconnect

Not our scope

  • Drywall, framing, finish carpentry
  • Plumbing rough or repair
  • HVAC install or service
  • Painting, flooring, tile
  • Roofing, siding, gutters
  • Generator install (gas hook-up side)
  • Low-voltage / structured cabling builds
  • Solar & battery systems
  • Anything 4-family-plus or commercial code
  • New construction GC work

What every job actually looks like

From the text you send us tonight to the moment we hand back your keys. Predictable on purpose.

01

You text us

A few photos and a sentence in your own words. You don't need to know what to call it. "The outlet in the kitchen is warm" is plenty.

02

We text back (same day)

Usually within a couple of hours, always within 24. If we need to see it in person before quoting, we'll book a short visit that's free.

03

Written quote in your inbox

Itemized. Flat-rate when we can, capped time-and-materials when we can't. Permit fees included. No "signs here for an unspecified amount."

04

We show up when we said

Text the morning of. Text again when we're parked, so nobody answers the door surprised. Shoes off when you ask. Drop cloths down.

05

Walkthrough before we leave

We walk you through what we did, what we found that wasn't in the quote (and why we didn't change it), and what to watch for. You sign off; the bill is exactly the quote.
