And: ("Owner")
Effective Date:

1. Scope of Services

Provider performs the following services for Owner's residential rental properties:

  • Routine maintenance and repairs
  • Emergency repairs
  • Tenant coordination and communication
  • Property inspections
  • Vendor and subcontractor management

Services outside this scope (including but not limited to tenant placement, lease negotiation, eviction proceedings, and rent collection) are not covered under this agreement.

2. Retainer

Owner maintains a rolling retainer balance with Provider. An initial deposit of $5,000 is due at signing.

When the retainer balance falls below $1,500, Owner agrees to replenish the retainer to $5,000 within 15 days of written notice. If replenishment is not received within 15 days, Provider may prioritize other commitments and response times may be extended until the retainer is replenished.

Retainer deposits and replenishments shall be made via ACH transfer, CashApp, or Zelle.

3. Billable Work

All work is billed against the retainer balance. Each task is classified as one of:

TypeBilling
FlatFixed price agreed before work begins
HourlyBilled at $35/hr per worker, tracked to the quarter hour
Emergency / After-HoursBilled at $70/hr per worker (see Section 4)

Time spent sourcing, purchasing, picking up, or delivering materials is billable at the standard hourly rate. Materials and subcontractor costs are billed at cost with no markup.

4. Emergency Work

Provider may perform emergency work without prior Owner approval when delay would cause property damage, safety risk, or code violation, up to $1,000 per incident. This includes but is not limited to water leaks, gas issues, electrical hazards, and loss of heat. Emergency work exceeding $1,000 requires Owner approval; however, Provider may proceed if Owner is unreachable within a reasonable time.

Owner will be notified within 24 hours of any emergency work performed. Emergency and after-hours work is billed at $70/hr per worker (double the standard rate).

5. Work Authorization

Tenants may report maintenance issues by contacting Owner directly (text or phone) or by contacting Provider at Owner's direction. Owner may also provide tenants with Provider's phone number for direct contact. Either way works — no change to how tenants currently communicate.

Non-emergency work estimated under $500 may be performed without prior Owner approval. For work estimated at $500 or above:

  • Tenant reports issue to Owner or Provider
  • Provider reviews and estimates (flat price or hourly estimate)
  • Provider confirms scope with Owner via text
  • Owner approves or denies
  • Provider performs work, logs completion, balance is deducted

As the online portal is completed, tenants will be able to submit requests directly through it. This transition will happen gradually and will not disrupt the existing text-based workflow.

6. Statements

Provider maintains a running record of all deposits, deductions, and work completed. Statements are available to Owner on request.

As the online portal is completed, Owner will have real-time access to balance, transaction history, and work records. Until then, Provider will generate statements as needed.

7. Expenses

All materials, subcontractor costs, and other expenses incurred in completing approved work are deducted from the retainer at cost — no markup. Time spent sourcing and procuring materials is billed as labor per Section 3. No additional approval is required beyond the original work order approval.

8. Documentation

Provider will photograph work before and after completion. Photos and records are available to Owner on request, and through the online portal once completed.

9. Communication

Provider will respond to non-emergency requests within 24 hours and emergency requests within 2 hours during business hours (Mon–Fri, 8am–6pm). After-hours emergency response is best effort.

10. Independent Contractor

Provider is an independent contractor and not an employee of Owner. Provider maintains their own tools, equipment, insurance, and workers. Provider controls the manner and means of performing work under this agreement.

Provider will obtain and maintain general liability insurance specific to this contract, purchased at signing and proof of coverage provided to Owner within 24 hours of the effective date. Provider's scope of work includes electrical work on residential properties up to 3-family (no license required per Hamilton County regulations), minor plumbing and HVAC work not requiring permits, and ductwork. Licensed plumbing and refrigeration work are excluded and will be subcontracted when needed.

11. Rate Changes

Provider may adjust hourly and flat rates with 30 days written notice to Owner.

12. Term

This agreement is month-to-month. Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice.

Upon termination, any remaining retainer balance will be refunded within 30 days, less any outstanding charges for work completed or in progress.

13. Portal Access

Provider maintains an online portal for Owner and tenants as part of this service at no additional charge. Portal access is available for the duration of this agreement and ends upon termination.

15. Property Access

Owner shall provide Provider with keys, codes, or other means of access to all properties and units covered under this agreement. If a tenant refuses or restricts access, Owner is responsible for resolving the issue. Provider is not liable for delays caused by inability to access a property.

16. Warranty

Provider warrants labor for 30 days from completion. If a repair fails within the warranty period due to workmanship, Provider will correct it at no additional charge. This warranty does not cover tenant damage, normal wear and tear, or pre-existing conditions.

17. Limitation of Liability

Provider's total liability under this agreement shall not exceed the current retainer balance at the time of any claim. Provider is not liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages, including but not limited to lost rental income.

18. Indemnification

Owner shall maintain adequate property and liability insurance on all covered properties for the duration of this agreement.

Owner agrees to hold Provider harmless from claims arising from pre-existing property conditions, tenant actions, or Owner's failure to maintain adequate insurance. Provider agrees to hold Owner harmless from claims arising directly from Provider's negligence in performing work under this agreement.

19. Non-Solicitation

During the term of this agreement and for 12 months following termination, Owner shall not directly hire or engage any subcontractor, laborer, or worker introduced to Owner through Provider's services.

20. Force Majeure

Neither party shall be liable for delays or failure to perform due to circumstances beyond their reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, severe weather, pandemics, supply chain disruptions, or government actions.

21. Dispute Resolution

In the event of a dispute, both parties agree to attempt resolution through informal negotiation first. If unresolved within 30 days, the parties agree to mediation before pursuing any legal action. Mediation costs shall be split equally.

22. Notices

Formal notices under this agreement (including replenishment requests, termination, and rate changes) may be delivered by text message, email, or U.S. mail to the contact information on file. Notice is considered received on the date sent for text/email, or 3 business days after mailing.

23. Governing Law

This agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Ohio. Any legal proceedings shall be conducted in Hamilton or Butler County, Ohio.

24. Entire Agreement

This document constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions, representations, or agreements, whether written or oral. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

25. Severability

If any provision of this agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
